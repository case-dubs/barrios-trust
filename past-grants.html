Grants Made in 2016

The Museum of Children’s Art

The Museum of Children’s Art (MOCHA) provides creative and unique arts learning experiences to Oakland Students through day, afterschool and summer programs. The Barrios Trust supported MOCHA's Cleveland Arts Residency Program, providing arts instructions to students and professional development for teachers at Oakland's Cleveland Elementary School.

Saint Vincent’s Day Home

Saint Vincent’s Day Home has been providing high quality early childhood and youth development programs to low-income families in West Oakland and surrounding neighborhoods for over 100 years. The Barrios Trust most recently made a grant to St. Vincent's to support general operating expenses.

Girls, Inc.

Girls, Inc. provides programs that inspire girls from kindergarten through high school to be strong, smart and bold. The Barrios Trust's most recent grant to Girls, Inc. supported the GIRLStart program at Lockwood and Parker Elementary schools in East Oakland.

Brothers On The Rise

Brothers On The Rise (BOTR) works to responsibly empower young men ages 6 to 17 to achieve personal, academic and professional success and to contribute to a just and equitable society. The Barrios Trust supported BOTR's Brothers Unite program, an afterschool and summer program for boys 4th to 8th grade.

Bay Area Outreach & Recreational Program

Bay Area Outreach & Recreational Program (BORP) provides sports programs for youth with physical disabilities which encourages the health, independence and social integration of the young participants. The Barrios Trust supported BORP's sled hockey and wheelchair basketball programs for youth with physical disabilities, ages 5-19.
